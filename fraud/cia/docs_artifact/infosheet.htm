Information Sheet on FAQs regarding Corporate Integrity Agreements

November 6, 2000

On May 30, 2000, the Office of Inspector General (OIG) issued guidance to assist health care providers operating under corporate integrity agreements or settlement agreements with integrity provisions (both of which will be referred to as "CIAs") in fulfilling their obligations to safeguard Federal health care programs from fraud and abuse. At that time, the guidance included a checklist for meeting CIA Annual Report requirements and answers to four frequently asked questions (FAQs) about CIAs regarding: 1) the reporting by providers under CIAs of Federal health care program overpayments; 2) the independence of an "independent review organization" (IRO) for purposes of a CIA review; 3) whether the OIG endorses or assists a provider in choosing a compliance vendor or IRO; and 4) the reporting of material violations as part of CIA reports.

Most CIAs require that a billing review be conducted, either by an IRO or in some cases internally by the provider with a verification review performed by the IRO. Over the past several years, the language describing these billing reviews has evolved from being quite general to fairly specific in more recent CIAs. As part of our continued effort to provide guidance to all providers under CIAs, and to further clarify the specific language that may appear in a provider's CIA, the OIG is today posting 23 new answers to questions frequently asked by providers or their IROs with respect to these billing reviews.

Today's posting will update the original listing and add a specific index of topics covered by these FAQs, as follows:

1. Reporting of Overpayments
2. Independence of an Independent Review Organization
3. Selecting an Independent Review Organization
4. Material Violations
5. CIA Billing Reviews
