HEADLINE: CLINICAL LABORATORY AGREES TO PAY $10.1 MILLION FOR FALSE MEDICARE CLAIMS

Inspector General June Gibbs Brown announced that Spectra Laboratories, Inc., a California clinical laboratory specializing in end-stage renal disease testing, agreed to pay $10.1 million to resolve civil liability under the False Claims Act, the Civil Monetary Penalties Law and the Medicare/Medicaid Anti-Kickback Law. Spectra submitted separate claims for laboratory tests to Medicare, the Railroad Retirement Board, the Civilian Health and Medical Program of the Uniformed Services and the Federal Employees Health Benefits Program when those programs had already paid the patients' dialysis centers for the tests under a composite rate. Spectra also fragmented the billing of automated hematology and chemistry tests.

In addition, Spectra used several schemes to induce payment of medically unnecessary laboratory tests, including a test-ordering system which allowed physicians to order all the same tests for all his patients, both now and in the future, regardless of whether they were needed. Another scheme involved a commission plan which compensated salespeople on the basis of Medicare reimbursements obtained from their accounts, as well as other marketing and promotion techniques and inducements to encourage the ordering of medically unnecessary tests.

As part of the settlement agreement, Spectra is to enter a comprehensive compliance program. The program requires, among other things, that Spectra implement a patient-specific test-ordering system and a tracking system to ensure accuracy and validity of future billings to Medicare and other Government health care programs. It also requires that Spectra revise its sales and marketing practices.
