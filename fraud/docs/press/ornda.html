SUBJECT: Hospital Firm Pays $12.6 Million to Settle Kickback Allegations

Inspector General June Gibbs Brown announced that OrNda Healthcorp has agreed to pay the Federal Government more than $12.6 million to settle allegations that several of its hospitals paid physicians for Medicare patient referrals and received referrals from physicians with whom it had improper financial relationships. The hospitals, which were primarily located in California, submitted Medicare claims for patients improperly referred by doctors who received kickbacks. Some physicians were paid as "directors" of hospital programs that did not exist; others were paid for services not needed or not performed.

OrNda, which is based in Tennessee, was acquired in January 1997 by California-based Tenet Healthcare Corporation, the second largest investor-owned health care services company in the country. Tenet is part of the settlement agreement only because it became the successor in interest to OrNda. The agreement contains no admission of guilt by either company, nor does it resolve any potential claims against the physicians involved.

The settlement resolves a law suit brought by a former surgeon of one of the hospitals, in which the Government elected to intervene. He will receive more than $2.3 million of the recovery, along with an additional sum to resolve personal claims against OrNda.
