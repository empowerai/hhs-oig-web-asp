HEADLINE: Chiropractor Sentenced in Fraud Scheme

Inspector General June Gibbs Brown announced that chiropractor Jaroslava Odvarko was sentenced in Iowa in relation to a scheme to defraud the Medicare and Medicaid programs and private insurance companies. Odvarko was sentenced to 1 year and a day in prison and ordered to pay restitution of $27,270 to private insurance companies she defrauded. She also signed a civil agreement to pay $60,000 to Medicare and Medicaid, which had overpaid her $30,000. In addition, Odvarko is subject to a 5-year exclusion from participating in Medicare and Medicaid programs.

In November 1996 Odvarko agreed to plead guilty to one count of mail fraud rather than face possible conviction on 40 counts of conspiracy, mail fraud, false claims, money laundering and criminal forfeiture. She had employees alter and falsify office records to make it appear patients received spinal manipulations or physical therapy when the service included only the sale of placebos, vitamins or nonreimbursable items and services. She later billed Medicare, Medicaid or the patient's insurance company for fictitious office visits to cover nonreimbursable amounts. She usually waited a year to bill for these visits on the theory that by then the patients would not remember whether or not they had been there.

This case was the result of a joint effort of the Office of Inspector General and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
