HEADLINE: Psychologist Convicted of Defrauding Medicare of $2.5 Million

Inspector General June Gibbs Brown announced that Viola Wiegand, a New York psychologist, was convicted on charges related to billing Medicare for $2.5 million in services she did not provide. Wiegand, who will be 77 years old next month, and four others concocted a scheme in which individuals were solicited to participate in disability insurance fraud.

Over a 2-year period Wiegand submitted more than 10,000 false Medicare claims. The false claims were part of a kickback scheme in which Wiegand paid individuals 25 percent of whatever insurers paid her, to feign automobile and workplace accidents. Subsequently, Wiegand made up false reports stating the individuals suffered from post-traumatic stress disorder, then billed repeatedly to treat their nonexistent illnesses. Eventually they would qualify for Social Security disability and Medicare, and she would sometimes bill for seeing as many as 35 of them a day for 60- to 90-minute treatments.

Because of Wiegand's behavioral problem during the trial, she was remanded to jail until her sentencing in August. One of the co-conspirators was convicted by the same jury for submitting false claims to insurance companies. The other three pled guilty earlier for participating in the scheme.
