HEADLINE: INSPECTOR GENERAL JUNE GIBBS BROWN NAMES INDUSTRY GUIDANCE CHIEF

Kevin G. McAnaney has been selected as the Chief, Industry Guidance Branch, Office of Counsel to the Inspector General, Department of Health and Human Services. In this position, he is responsible for advisory opinions, safe harbors and Special Fraud Alerts issued by the Office of Inspector General. Mr. McAnaney has been a partner at the law firm of Dewey Ballantine for over 10 years, with a broad and diversified health law practice.

Mr. McAnaney graduated from Columbia Law School in 1977 and began his health law career as an associate at the firm of Kelley, Drye and Warren in New York, where he also served as in-house counsel to New York Hospital. In 1980, he moved on to the position of Assistant Counsel to the Governor (New York), where he participated in designing the Governor's health legislative program. In 1983, he joined the Washington, D.C. office of Dewey Ballantine and became a partner in 1986. His practice has included regulatory advice to health care providers, including fraud and abuse, reimbursement and licensure issues. He also defended physicians in disciplinary and related proceedings.

Mr. McAnaney's primary task will be implementation of the advisory opinion mechanism enacted as part of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996. Beginning February 21, 1997, the Office of Inspector General (OIG) will commence consideration of requests for advisory opinions on questions related to many of OIG's civil monetary penalty, exclusion and criminal law enforcement issues.
