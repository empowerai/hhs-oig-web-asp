HEADLINE: Mental Health Care Executive Pled Guilty in Medicare Fraud Scheme

Inspector General June Gibbs Brown announced that Bern Anderson, a mental health care executive formerly of Washington, D.C., pled guilty in Massachusetts to defrauding the Department, six States and the District of Columbia of an estimated $2.5 million in Medicaid and State mental retardation program funds. Anderson was charged with conspiracy and fraud related to mental health care for de-institutionalized mentally retarded individuals in eight non-profit mental health care facilities. From 1985 to 1992 Anderson and five co-conspirators participated in a scheme to defraud the non-profit facilities through a for-profit management services company.

Anderson and co-conspirators Louis Gallinaro, C. Thomas Campagna, Eugene Karmelek, Paul Campagna, and Albert Dias operated a for-profit management services company which primarily managed the non-profit facilities in Rhode Island, New Hampshire, Massachusetts, Connecticut, New Jersey, Virginia and Washington, D.C. Anderson and the co-conspirators devised a scheme in which they set up fictitious companies to falsely bill the non-profit facilities to receive kickbacks for goods and services furnished to the centers. They also billed for services never furnished which caused the centers to pay excessive management fees and caused them to do business with for-profit companies Anderson and the co-conspirators owned. The five co-conspirators pled guilty in 1994 and 1995 and agreed to cooperate in the case against Anderson.

This case was the result of a joint effort of the Office of Inspector General, the U.S. Postal Inspection Service and the Massachusetts State auditor's office.
