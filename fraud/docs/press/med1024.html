HEADLINE: TWO SENTENCED IN MULTI-MILLION DOLLAR FRAUD SCHEME

Inspector General June Gibbs Brown announced that two Tennessee men were sentenced in Massachusetts in connection with a multi-million dollar scheme to defraud Medicare. Gary Lakins, owner and president of Providers Inc., a Tennessee-based company, was sentenced to 37 months imprisonment and ordered to pay $10,000 in restitution. Randy Jenkins, a company official, received 2 years probation and was told to pay restitution of $1,000. Three other company managers pled guilty and were sentenced earlier in the fraudulent scheme that caused an estimated loss of $5 million to the Medicare program.

Providers, a durable medical equipment company, billed carriers in several States, including Massachusetts, for incontinence care items supposedly supplied to patients in nursing homes and long-term care facilities. The defendants billed at inflated rates, billed for supplies not sold, backdated requests for payment from Medicare, and withheld from Medicare refunds owed for supplies ordered for deceased patients. Providers engaged in "carrier shopping," setting up shell offices and mail drops across the country, billing where the highest rate for an item could be obtained. While the other defendants received little profit from the fraud beyond their salaries, Lakins received more than $1 million from Providers during the course of the conspiracy.

The investigation of this case was a joint effort of the Office of Inspector General with the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the Defense Criminal Investigation Services and the Railroad Retirement Board.
