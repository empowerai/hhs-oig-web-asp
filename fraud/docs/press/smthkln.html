HEADLINE: Clinical Laboratory Agrees to Pay $325 Million to Settle False Medicare Claims

The United States Attorney's Office for the District of Pennsylvania, the Office of the Inspector General (OIG) of the Department of Health and Human Services, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the Postal Inspection Service, the Defense Criminal Investigative Service and the Railroad Retirement Board announced that SmithKline Beecham Clinical Laboratories, Inc., has agreed to pay $325 million in settlement of civil claims. SmithKline also agreed to implement stringent compliance requirements to settle its civil liability for filing false claims for reimbursement to the Medicare, Medicaid, Civilian Health and Medical Program of the Uniformed Services, and Railroad Retirement Board health care programs.

SmithKline, a target of a multi-agency laboratory initiative, engaged in many improper activities. These activities included: unbundling clinical laboratory tests; billing for tests not performed; inserting false diagnosis codes to obtain reimbursement; paying kickbacks to physicians for patient referrals; double-billing for laboratory tests for patients with end stage renal disease; and billing for calculations which were both unordered and medically unnecessary.

This settlement also resolves several qui tam lawsuits alleging these violations. A separate qui tam raised allegations of test data manipulation and falsified test results, but an independent review found only minor quality problems with no adverse effect on patient care. This settlement does not resolve any potential criminal charges against SmithKline or any individuals.

This is one of the two largest civil health care fraud settlements ever reached in the United States Government. It is also the largest settlement of any case involving claims under the qui tam provisions of the False Claims Act.
