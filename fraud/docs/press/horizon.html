HEADLINE: Horizon/CMS Healthcare Corporation Pays a Total of $5.8 Million to Resolve Charges of False Medicare Claims

Inspector General June Gibbs Brown, Office of Inspector General (OIG) for the Department of Health and Human Services and the Department of Justice (DOJ) announced that Horizon/CMS Healthcare Corporation has paid a total of $5.8 million to resolve civil liability for false claims. Horizon, a national provider of nursing care and rehabilitation services in Albuquerque, New Mexico and employer of 40,000 people, paid $4.6 million to DOJ to settle the allegations. Earlier, Horizon returned nearly $1.2 million when it learned about the OIG investigation.

In 1994 Horizon bought a chain of 19 rehabilitation facilities, which it found had not billed for ancillary medical supplies. Believing it had found an unclaimed income source, Horizon billed Medicare, Medicaid and other insurers for supplies which might have been used in various services. However, Horizon's billing and accounting staff lacked adequate training. Horizon misused and misinterpreted the records, which caused it to bill for multiple medical supplies. For example, since tracheostomy care is usually given 3 times a day, Horizon billed for 93 trach tubes, 93 trach care kits and other related items, per beneficiary, for each 30-day period. Intermediary personnel indicated that one trach tube a year was normal. As a result, from July to December 1994, the company billed Medicare $3.4 million for medical supplies and was paid $1.2 million.

Treble damages were assessed because of Horizon's disregard of proper billing requirements and of inquiries by employees. However, Horizon cooperated fully in the investigation and agreed to enter a corporate integrity program. The program requires that Horizon appoint a subcommittee, conduct ongoing orientation and training sessions, and perform regular audits and assessments to ensure billing accuracy.

The investigation of this case was a joint effort of OIG and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
