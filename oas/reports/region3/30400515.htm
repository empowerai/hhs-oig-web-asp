Department of Health and Human Services

Office of Inspector General -- AUDIT

"Pennsylvania Protection & Advocacy, Inc. Procurement Practices and Potential Conflicts of Interest During Fiscal Years 2001 Through 2003," (A-03-04-00515)

July 10, 2007

Complete Text of Report is available in PDF format (635 kb). Copies can also be obtained by contacting the Office of Public Affairs at 202-619-1343.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:

The objectives of our review of Pennsylvania Protection & Advocacy, Inc. (PP&A) were to assess its procurement practices, assess potential conflicts of interest, and evaluate PP&A's monitoring of its contractors.  PP&A had contracting practices that did not reflect the use of open competition as required by Federal law; had two situations that involved conflicts of interest in violation of Federal law; and did not comply with Federal regulations in monitoring its legal services contractors because it did not verify that contractor costs billed were allowable, allocable, and reasonable.

We recommended that PP&A (1) use open competition in procuring legal services contracts or stop sole source contracting and establish in-house legal services expertise; (2) require its executive director to terminate her conflicting employment relationship; (3) decline acceptance of future contributions from any of its contractors; and (4) ensure that it complies with Federal regulations in monitoring contractor billings.  PP&A concurred with our recommendations, but disagreed with the repayment part of the third recommendation, saying that its recent merger with the contractor would nullify repayment.  Accordingly, we modified our third recommendation.
